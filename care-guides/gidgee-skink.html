Gidgee Skink Care Guide

Egernia stokesii

Terrestrial-rock dweller Can be social Moderate humidity

This page summarizes practical baseline husbandry targets for Gidgee Skink. Individual animals can vary, so always adjust based on behavior, body condition, and veterinary guidance.

Enclosure Requirements

  • Recommended minimum: 4x2x2 ft for pair/group setups.
  • Provide stacked cork/rock-like crevices and stable secure hides.
  • Offer multiple basking and retreat options to reduce social pressure.

Temperature and Humidity Requirements

  • Ambient: 75-85 F (24-29 C).
  • Basking area: 100-110 F (38-43 C).
  • Night drop: near 68-75 F (20-24 C).
  • Humidity: generally 30-50 percent with access to a more humid microhide as needed.

Diet and Supplementation Requirements

  • Omnivorous tendency: offer insects plus appropriate plant matter.
  • Rotate feeders and avoid fatty feeder overuse.
  • Calcium and vitamin supplementation should match UVB and diet composition.

Handling and Behavioral Management

  • Can form social groups; monitor for bullying and resource guarding.
  • Provide enough hiding and feeding stations for all animals.
  • Handle calmly and keep sessions short while trust develops.

Health Monitoring and Safety

  • Watch for bite marks or condition loss in lower-ranking animals.
  • Check sheds and toes regularly, especially in drier periods.
  • Routine weight tracking helps detect subtle group dynamic issues.

Red Rocks Exotic Reptiles care information is educational support only. If you notice illness, injury, breathing issues, neurological signs, or prolonged appetite loss, contact a reptile-experienced veterinarian immediately.
