Green Keel-Bellied Lizard Care Guide

Gastropholis prasina

Arboreal Fast and alert Humidity-sensitive

This page summarizes practical baseline husbandry targets for Green Keel-Bellied Lizard. Individual animals can vary, so always adjust based on behavior, body condition, and veterinary guidance.

Enclosure Requirements

  • Minimum enclosure: 24x24x36 in for one adult; larger for pairs.
  • Use a highly planted vertical environment with branches and visual barriers.
  • Offer secure basking perches and shaded retreat zones.

Temperature and Humidity Requirements

  • Day ambient: 75-82 F (24-28 C).
  • Basking area: 90-95 F (32-35 C).
  • Night: 70-75 F (21-24 C).
  • Humidity: 60-80 percent with airflow to reduce stagnant moisture.

Diet and Supplementation Requirements

  • Feed varied gut-loaded insects as the core diet.
  • Juveniles generally require more frequent feedings than adults.
  • Dust prey with calcium and use a multivitamin schedule tailored to UVB exposure.

Handling and Behavioral Management

  • Very quick and visual; stress rises with abrupt enclosure disturbance.
  • Use calm, predictable routines and limit long handling sessions.
  • Provide enrichment through climbing complexity and varied perch diameters.

Health Monitoring and Safety

  • Common risk factors include dehydration and stress-related anorexia.
  • Monitor body condition and stool quality weekly.
  • Promptly address any prolonged inactivity, closed-eye posture, or breathing effort.

Red Rocks Exotic Reptiles care information is educational support only. If you notice illness, injury, breathing issues, neurological signs, or prolonged appetite loss, contact a reptile-experienced veterinarian immediately.
