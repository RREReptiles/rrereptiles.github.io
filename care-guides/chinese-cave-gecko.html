Chinese Cave Gecko Care Guide

Goniurosaurus hainanensis

Nocturnal Cool-temperature species High hide usage

This page summarizes practical baseline husbandry targets for Chinese Cave Gecko. Individual animals can vary, so always adjust based on behavior, body condition, and veterinary guidance.

Enclosure Requirements

  • Minimum enclosure around 20-gallon long equivalent for one adult; larger is preferred.
  • Use multiple tight hides, including at least one humid hide.
  • Keep setup cluttered and low-stress with minimal bright exposure.

Temperature and Humidity Requirements

  • Cool ambient target: about 68-75 F (20-24 C).
  • Warm hide/spot: around 78-82 F (26-28 C).
  • Avoid high heat; prolonged hot conditions can be dangerous.
  • Humidity: 60-80 percent with humid retreat area near 80-90 percent.

Diet and Supplementation Requirements

  • Feed appropriately sized gut-loaded insects (roaches, crickets, larvae).
  • Adults commonly feed 2-3 times weekly; juveniles need more frequency.
  • Supplement with calcium and vitamins on a planned schedule.

Handling and Behavioral Management

  • Generally shy and best managed with observation-focused care.
  • Limit handling frequency to reduce stress.
  • Nocturnal activity patterns are normal; daytime hiding is expected.

Health Monitoring and Safety

  • Dehydration and shed issues are common when humidity is inconsistent.
  • Observe tail condition, stool quality, and feeding response.
  • Quarantine and parasite screening are recommended for all new arrivals.

Red Rocks Exotic Reptiles care information is educational support only. If you notice illness, injury, breathing issues, neurological signs, or prolonged appetite loss, contact a reptile-experienced veterinarian immediately.
