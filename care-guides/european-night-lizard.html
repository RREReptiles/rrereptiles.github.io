European Night Lizard Care Guide

Lepidophyma flavimaculatum

Secretive Humidity-sensitive Low handling

This page summarizes practical baseline husbandry targets for European Night Lizard. Individual animals can vary, so always adjust based on behavior, body condition, and veterinary guidance.

Taxonomy note: The scientific name listed here, Lepidophyma flavimaculatum, is also commonly referred to as the Yellow-Spotted Night Lizard.

Accuracy note: this is a general reference and not a substitute for veterinary diagnosis. For full breeder-specific protocol, use the full guide option below.

Enclosure Requirements

  • Provide a secure enclosure with layered cover, bark slabs, and tight retreats.
  • Prioritize microhabitats over open display space.
  • Ensure escape-proof seams and lid security.

Temperature and Humidity Requirements

  • Moderate temperatures are generally preferred: low-mid 70s F ambient with a gentle warm zone in the low 80s F.
  • Avoid overheating and sudden swings.
  • Humidity is typically moderate-high with ventilated airflow and localized moist retreats.

Diet and Supplementation Requirements

  • Feed appropriately sized invertebrates, gut-loaded and rotated for variety.
  • Adjust frequency by age and body condition response.
  • Use a calcium/vitamin supplementation routine appropriate to lighting and diet.

Handling and Behavioral Management

  • Usually shy and crepuscular/nocturnal in activity.
  • Minimal handling is recommended for long-term stability.
  • Frequent hiding is normal and should not be interpreted as poor health by itself.

Health Monitoring and Safety

  • Monitor hydration, shed quality, and body condition trend.
  • Track stool output and feeding response consistently.
  • Consult a reptile-experienced veterinarian when appetite changes persist.
