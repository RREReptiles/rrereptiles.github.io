Ebenaui Gecko Care Guide

Uroplatus ebenaui

Leaf-tailed gecko Cool-temperature Humidity dependent

This page summarizes practical baseline husbandry targets for Ebenaui Gecko. Individual animals can vary, so always adjust based on behavior, body condition, and veterinary guidance.

Enclosure Requirements

  • Use a vertically oriented naturalistic enclosure with bark and dense cover.
  • Provide multiple resting surfaces and visual barriers.
  • Keep disturbance low and prioritize stable microclimates.

Temperature and Humidity Requirements

  • Daytime often kept around 70-78 F (21-26 C).
  • Night drop to roughly 65-72 F (18-22 C) is common.
  • Humidity generally 70-90 percent with good airflow to prevent stagnation.

Diet and Supplementation Requirements

  • Offer varied appropriately sized insects with routine gut-loading.
  • Adults commonly fed several times per week; adjust to body condition.
  • Supplement calcium and vitamins based on schedule and UVB plan.

Handling and Behavioral Management

  • Highly cryptic species; often stressed by frequent handling.
  • Best results usually come from observation-first care.
  • Maintain consistent enclosure routines and low traffic.

Health Monitoring and Safety

  • Retained shed and dehydration are frequent husbandry concerns.
  • Monitor body condition and activity after lights-out.
  • Any prolonged anorexia warrants quick husbandry review and veterinary input.

Red Rocks Exotic Reptiles care information is educational support only. If you notice illness, injury, breathing issues, neurological signs, or prolonged appetite loss, contact a reptile-experienced veterinarian immediately.
