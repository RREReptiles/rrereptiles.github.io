Sarasinorum Gecko Care Guide

Correlophus sarasinorum

Arboreal Nocturnal Humidity-cycling

This page summarizes practical baseline husbandry targets for Sarasinorum Gecko. Individual animals can vary, so always adjust based on behavior, body condition, and veterinary guidance.

Accuracy note: this is a general reference and not a substitute for veterinary diagnosis. For full breeder-specific protocol, use the full guide option below.

Enclosure Requirements

  • Minimum enclosure: 18x18x24 in for one adult; larger for pairs.
  • Provide vertical climbing space and secure hides among branches/plants.
  • Maintain visual barriers to reduce stress in shared enclosures.

Temperature and Humidity Requirements

  • Typical range: 72-80 F (22-27 C).
  • Avoid overheating beyond low 80s F for long periods.
  • Humidity cycle similar to many New Caledonian geckos: moderate by day, higher at night.

Diet and Supplementation Requirements

  • Use complete gecko diet as staple; offer insects as supplemental feeders.
  • Adjust feeding frequency by age, breeding status, and body condition.
  • Use calcium and multivitamin support on a routine schedule.

Handling and Behavioral Management

  • Can be defensive at first; confidence improves with calm consistency.
  • Short low-stress handling sessions are preferred.
  • Track social compatibility closely in pair/group setups.

Health Monitoring and Safety

  • Monitor shed quality, appetite, and body composition monthly.
  • Address persistent lethargy or prolonged food refusal quickly.
  • Maintain clean water and a hygienic enclosure baseline.

Red Rocks Exotic Reptiles care information is educational support only. If you notice illness, injury, breathing issues, neurological signs, or prolonged appetite loss, contact a reptile-experienced veterinarian immediately.
