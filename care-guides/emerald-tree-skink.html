Emerald Tree Skink Care Guide

Lamprolepis smaragdina

Arboreal Active and social Moderate-high humidity

This page summarizes practical baseline husbandry targets for Emerald Tree Skink. Individual animals can vary, so always adjust based on behavior, body condition, and veterinary guidance.

Enclosure Requirements

  • Minimum enclosure: 18x18x24 in for one adult; 24x24x36 in or larger for pairs/groups.
  • Prioritize vertical climbing structure, cork, branches, and dense visual cover.
  • Provide multiple secure hides at different heights.

Temperature and Humidity Requirements

  • Day ambient: 75-82 F (24-28 C).
  • Basking zone: 88-92 F (31-33 C).
  • Night drop: 72-76 F (22-24 C).
  • Humidity: 60-80 percent with daily misting and a dry-out period between heavy sprays.

Diet and Supplementation Requirements

  • Offer gut-loaded insects (roaches, crickets, black soldier fly larvae) as staple feeders.
  • Juveniles: feed most days; adults: 3-4 feedings weekly depending on condition.
  • Use calcium regularly and a multivitamin on a rotating schedule.

Handling and Behavioral Management

  • Generally curious and often tolerate routine interaction when trust is built gradually.
  • Avoid force-handling; let them approach and step on your hand voluntarily.
  • Group setups require careful observation for dominance or feeding competition.

Health Monitoring and Safety

  • Watch for dehydration, retained shed on toes, and unexplained weight loss.
  • Maintain strict enclosure hygiene and quarantine new arrivals.
  • Seek reptile-veterinary care for persistent appetite, stool, or respiratory changes.

Red Rocks Exotic Reptiles care information is educational support only. If you notice illness, injury, breathing issues, neurological signs, or prolonged appetite loss, contact a reptile-experienced veterinarian immediately.
