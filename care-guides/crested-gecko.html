Crested Gecko Care Guide

Correlophus ciliatus

Arboreal Beginner-friendly Nocturnal

This page summarizes practical baseline husbandry targets for Crested Gecko. Individual animals can vary, so always adjust based on behavior, body condition, and veterinary guidance.

Accuracy note: this is a general reference and not a substitute for veterinary diagnosis. For full breeder-specific protocol, use the full guide option below.

Enclosure Requirements

  • Minimum enclosure: 18x18x24 in for one adult; larger is preferred.
  • Provide vertical climbing opportunities, cork, and hides.
  • Use a substrate and drainage strategy that supports humidity cycles safely.

Temperature and Humidity Requirements

  • Ideal range: 72-78 F (22-26 C).
  • Avoid prolonged temperatures above 82 F (28 C).
  • Humidity cycle: around 50-60 percent daytime and 70-80 percent at night.

Diet and Supplementation Requirements

  • Use a complete crested gecko diet as staple nutrition.
  • Offer appropriately sized insects 1-2 times weekly for enrichment and protein.
  • Provide calcium support based on feeder and lighting strategy.

Handling and Behavioral Management

  • Typically tolerant of short gentle handling once settled.
  • Do not handle immediately after feeding or during difficult sheds.
  • A stable routine reduces stress and improves feeding consistency.

Health Monitoring and Safety

  • Track body weight and tail-base condition over time.
  • Watch for retained shed on toes and persistent appetite drop.
  • Maintain clean water and enclosure sanitation to reduce infection risk.

Red Rocks Exotic Reptiles care information is educational support only. If you notice illness, injury, breathing issues, neurological signs, or prolonged appetite loss, contact a reptile-experienced veterinarian immediately.
