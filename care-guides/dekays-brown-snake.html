Dekays Brown Snake Care Guide

Storeria dekayi

Small colubrid Secretive Specialized feeder tendencies

This page summarizes practical baseline husbandry targets for Dekays Brown Snake. Individual animals can vary, so always adjust based on behavior, body condition, and veterinary guidance.

Enclosure Requirements

  • Use a secure, low-stress enclosure with many tight hides and leaf-litter style cover.
  • Provide moisture-retentive retreat zones plus drier resting space.
  • Escape-proof setup is essential due to small body size.

Temperature and Humidity Requirements

  • Ambient: approximately 72-78 F (22-26 C).
  • Warm zone: around 82-85 F (28-29 C).
  • Humidity: often 50-70 percent depending on ventilation and shed response.

Diet and Supplementation Requirements

  • Commonly takes soft-bodied invertebrates (for example worms/slugs depending on individual and legal availability).
  • Feeding response can be inconsistent; monitor body condition closely.
  • Because this species can be challenging in captivity, advanced keeper planning is recommended.

Handling and Behavioral Management

  • Generally shy and stress-sensitive; prioritize observation over handling.
  • Avoid frequent disturbance and enclosure rearrangement.
  • Provide visual cover to reduce defensive behavior.

Health Monitoring and Safety

  • Hydration, stool, and body condition should be tracked frequently.
  • Difficulty feeding is a common husbandry challenge.
  • Seek veterinary support early if feeding consistency drops.
